Exhibit 06 · Seventy years, one pattern

They were told.
And told. And told.

Zoom out and the shape appears. This was never one town or one decade. Between the first victim in this file and the most recent sits a chain of moments when the country was told — and what happened next. Which was, mostly, nothing.

1955

Where the evidence begins

The earliest abuse documented in the report's records dates to the mid-1950s. Seventy years separate the first victim in this file from the most recent. Both were children. Both were failed.

1997–2013

Rotherham

The years later examined by the Jay Report: at least 1,400 children abused in a single town while — the report records — the authorities paid to protect them looked away, and shredded what they knew.

2014

The Jay Report

Professor Alexis Jay puts Rotherham on the official record. The country is told, in an official document, what survivors had been saying for years. The pattern continues anyway.

2022

IICSA and Telford

The national Independent Inquiry into Child Sexual Abuse concludes. The Telford Inquiry finds more than 1,000 children abused over decades — same method, same institutional blindness. Told again.

2025

The Casey Audit

Baroness Casey's national audit finds the true scale "impossible to quantify" — because the data was never properly recorded. Not lost. Never recorded. A statutory inquiry is promised, again.

2025

This Inquiry

Unwilling to wait, more than 20,000 donors fund the Rape Gang Inquiry themselves — non-statutory, survivor-led, chaired by Rupert Lowe MP, with Sammy Woodhouse leading the team. Nothing ruled out of scope.

2026

This file — and after

The first phase concludes with the report this site reproduces: 149 districts named, the institutional record laid bare, full testimonies and legal action promised. One survivor's words stand for the whole timeline: "We're still having this conversation. We're still trying to get people to talk about it."

What was already known

The law & the prior inquiries.

The statutory framework, the safeguarding programmes, and the official inquiries that came before this one — the paper trail proving the state was told, in writing, again and again.

Never again — this time mean it.
