Evidence dies in drawers. This site is served from one folder — save it and it works offline. The data underneath is exportable in open formats. Copy it, archive it, hand it on.

Provenance & method. Built from the two files published alongside the report: the structured data extract (JSON) and the full report text. Five malformed fields in the JSON were repaired (unquoted values quoted; no content changed). The text was decoded from its original encoding and ligatures broken in conversion were restored ("tra?cking" → "trafficking"); no wording was added, removed or altered. Aggregates and the evidence web are computed directly from the structured records; where a record gives an age range, the earliest age is used. The "who did it" tallies group the report's own free-text perpetrator descriptions by keyword and count victim records mentioning each — an approximation, disclosed here. Individuals whose status is recorded as "not convicted" are named in testimony or by the report — inclusion reflects the source document and is not a legal finding. The blunt language across this site is presentation; every underlying claim is the report's, and the unedited source is on the "Source" page.
